## Légende des symboles

### Données spatiales complètes (Elmer)
- **zₛ(x,y,t_DEM)** : Surface topography at DEM dates
- **z_b(x,y)** : Bedrock topography
- **τ_b(x,y,t_DEM)** : Basal shear stress from Elmer
- **u_def(x,y,t_DEM)** : Deformation velocity from Elmer

### Observations aux stakes (séries temporelles)
- **H(t)** : Ice thickness (continuous, observed)
- **u_surf(t)** : Surface velocity (continuous, observed)

### Variables interpolées
- **τ_b(t)** : Basal stress at all observation dates
- **u_def(t)** : Deformation velocity at all observation dates
- **u_bed(t)** : Basal sliding velocity = u_surf(t) - u_def(t)

### Paramètres
- **m, As** : Weertman friction parameters
- **q, CN** : Lliboutry friction parameters
- **c₁, c₂** : Empirical relation coefficients
- **n** : Glen's flow law exponent (= 3)

## Points clés à mettre dans le texte

1. **Pas de calibration avec vitesses** :
   > "Elmer/Ice simulations are purely diagnostic: no velocity observations 
   > are used to constrain the model. The geometry (DEMs) alone determines 
   > the modeled stress and velocity fields."

2. **Rôle d'Eq. 7** :
   > "Equations 7a-b serve as temporal interpolators: they establish 
   > empirical relationships between ice thickness (continuously observed) 
   > and Elmer outputs (available only at DEM dates), enabling continuous 
   > reconstruction of τ_b(t) and u_def(t)."

3. **Stratégie complémentaire** :
   > "The approach combines the spatial completeness of numerical modeling 
   > with the temporal resolution of field observations."
